Whitespace

If you are paying attention, you'll notice that on Lesson 2, the System.out.print is indented. This is possible through a whitespace or simply a space.

Its sole purpose is to make our code indented and neat. Also, It does not hold any bearing in the program unless it is inside a System.out.println or System.out.print .

Another thing to take note, System.out.print("Yah" + " Yah");

There is an addition (+) operater, it can be used to concatenate/combine strings together. Well anyways, that was just additional information, In this lesson you will mix the use of System.out.println() and System.out.print().

Don't forget, to end your statements with a semicolon (;).
